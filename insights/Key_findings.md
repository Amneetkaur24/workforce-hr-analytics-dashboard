# Key Findings – HR Analytics Dashboard

## Workforce Overview

The organization currently has **7,984 active employees**, with a total of **8,950 employees hired** and **966 employees terminated**. This indicates overall workforce growth while maintaining a relatively controlled termination rate.

The hiring trend suggests consistent workforce expansion to support operational and business functions.

---

## Department Workforce Distribution

Employee distribution across departments reveals the following workforce concentration:

• **Operations – 2,429 employees**
• **Sales – 1,634 employees**
• **Customer Service – 1,489 employees**
• **IT – 1,243 employees**
• **Marketing – 648 employees**
• **Finance – 389 employees**
• **HR – Smaller workforce segment**

Operations represents the largest department, indicating that operational activities form the backbone of the organization.

---

## Workforce Demographics

Gender distribution across the organization is relatively balanced:

• **Male Employees – 54%**  
• **Female Employees – 46%**

This indicates a near balanced workforce representation with a slightly higher proportion of male employees.

---

## Age Distribution Insights

The age distribution shows a strong concentration of employees in the **35–44 age group**, followed by the **45–54 age group**.

This suggests that the workforce largely consists of **mid-career professionals**, which typically reflects experienced talent contributing to operational stability and business performance.

---

## Education & Salary Trends

Salary distribution increases with higher levels of education:

• **High School:** ~$63K average salary  
• **Bachelor's Degree:** ~$66K – $74K  
• **Master's Degree:** ~$80K – $86K  
• **PhD:** ~$80K – $93K  

This indicates a **positive correlation between education level and compensation**, which aligns with common workforce compensation structures.

---

## Geographic Distribution

Employee locations are distributed between **headquarters (HQ)** and **branch locations**, with the majority of employees located at HQ.

This reflects a centralized organizational structure where core operations and management functions are primarily handled at the main location.

---

## Workforce Retention Patterns

Employee length-of-employment analysis shows several employees with **long tenures ranging between 7–10 years**, suggesting relatively stable retention among experienced employees.

However, newer hires with shorter tenure indicate ongoing recruitment and workforce expansion.

---

## Key Business Insight

The dashboard reveals that:

• Operational roles dominate workforce distribution  
• Mid-career professionals form the majority of employees  
• Higher education correlates with higher salary bands  
• Workforce composition is relatively balanced across genders  

These insights can support **HR planning, workforce strategy, and compensation analysis**.
